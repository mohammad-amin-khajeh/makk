## appeler or s'appeler?

- ### appeler

  appeler directly translates as 'to call' and is used when one person calls another person or thing.
  It can also be used in a variety of contexts,
  such as to summon someone or something, to make a phone call, or to give a name.

  | French Sentence | English translation |
  | -------------- | --------------- |
  |  Je vais **appeler** Paul au téléphone.  | {{ spoiler(text="I'm going to call Paul on the phone.") }} |
  |  Peux-tu **appeler** un taxi?  | {{ spoiler(text="Can you call a taxi?") }} |
  | Ils vont **appeler** leur fille Élise. | {{ spoiler(text="They are going to name their daughter Elise.") }} |

- ### S'appeler

  S'appeler is a reflexive form of appeler and means 'to be called' or 'to call oneself'.
  It is used when the subject is also the object, i.e. when referring to oneself, as in saying one's name.

  | French Sentence | English translation |
  | -------------- | --------------- |
  | Comment **t'appelles**-tu ? | {{ spoiler(text="What is your name?") }} |
  | Elle **s'appelle** Marie. | {{ spoiler(text="Her name is Marie") }} |
  |Nous nous **appelons** Jean et Luc. | {{ spoiler(text="Our names are Jean and Luc") }} |

- ### conjugation table

  | Singular               | Plural                |
  |-----------------------|----------------------|
  | Je m'appelle          | Nous nous appelons    |
  | Tu t'appelles         | Vous vous appelez     |
  | Il/Elle s'appelle     | Ils/Elles s'appellent |

  In summary, while both appeler and s'appeler refer to the concept of calling,
  appeler is used for calling others or naming things,
  whereas s'appeler refers to what someone is called or calling oneself by name.

## habiter

- ### conjugation table

  | Singular               | Plural                |
  |-----------------------|----------------------|
  | J'habite              | Nous habitons        |
  | Tu habites            | Vous habitez         |
  | Il/Elle habite        | Ils/Elles habitent   |

- ### Example sentences

  | French Sentence | English Translation |
  |---|---|
  | **J'habite** à Paris. | {{spoiler(text="I live in Paris.")}} |
  | **Nous habitons** dans une maison. | {{spoiler(text="We live in a house.")}} |
  | **Ils habitent** au Canada. | {{spoiler(text="They live in Canada.")}} |
  | **Où habitez-vous** ? | {{spoiler(text="Where do you live?")}} |
  | **Elle habite** avec ses parents. | {{spoiler(text="She lives with her parents.")}} |
  | **Tu habites** près de l'école. | {{spoiler(text="You live near the school.")}} |

- ### Country names along with the correct preposition

  | French Country Name | English Translation |
  |---|---|
  | **au** Canada | Canada |
  | **aux** États-Unis | the United States |
  | **en** France | France |
  | **en** Italie | Italy |
  | **en** Iran | Iran |
  | **en** Espagne | Spain |
  | **en** Allemagne | Germany |
  | **en** Chine | Chine |

## épeler

This verb means to spell and it is mostly used to ask someone to spell their name as in:

| French Sentence | English translation |
| -------------- | --------------- |
| Comment **épelez**-vous votre nom? | {{ spoiler(text="How do you spell your name?") }} |

- ### Conjugation table

  | Singular          | Plural            |
  |-------------------|-------------------|
  | j'épelle          | nous épelons      |
  | tu épelles        | vous épelez       |
  | il/elle épelle | ils/elles épellent|

## Aller

**Aller** is a very common French verb meaning "to go".
It's one of the irregular verbs in French, so its conjugation is different from regular verbs.

- ### Conjugation Table

  | Singular          | Plural            |
  |-------------------|-------------------|
  | je vais          | nous allons      |
  | tu vas          | vous allez        |
  | il/elle va   | ils/elles vont |

- ### Example sentences

  | French Sentence | English Translation |
  |---|---|
  | **Je vais** au cinéma. | {{spoiler(text="I am going to the cinema.")}} |
  | **Tu vas** à la plage. | {{spoiler(text="You are going to the beach.")}} |
  | **Il va** au restaurant. | {{spoiler(text="He is going to the restaurant.")}} |
  | **Nous allons** à la fête. | {{spoiler(text="We are going to the party.")}} |
  | **Vous allez** au parc. | {{spoiler(text="You are going to the park.")}} |
  | **Ils vont** à l'école. | {{spoiler(text="They are going to school.")}} |
  | **Tu vas** bien? | {{spoiler(text="How are you?/Are you okay?")}} |

{{admonition(type='note'
text='The verb "aller" is also used to express future actions in French.
For example, "Je **vais** manger" means "I am going to eat."')}}

## quel and quelle

**Quel** and **quelle** are French interrogative adjectives used to ask questions about nouns.
Like "which" or "what" in English.

- **Quel:** Used before masculine singular nouns.
- **Quelle:** Used before feminine singular nouns.

- ### Sentence examples

  | French Sentence | English Translation |
  |---|---|
  | **Quelle** est votre profession? | What do you do for a living? |
  | **Quel** est ton/votre nom ? | What's your name? |
  | **Quelle** est votre adresse? | What's your address? |
  | **Quel** est votre numéro de téléphone?| What's your telephone number?|
